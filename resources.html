What Is Implicit Bias?

Implicit bias refers to unconscious attitudes or stereotypes that affect how we perceive and interact with others. These biases are shaped by culture, media, and lived experience, and they can influence decisions even when we believe ourselves to be fair.

Intersectionality

Intersectionality describes how different aspects of identity—such as race, gender, class, and ability—overlap and shape lived experiences. Understanding intersectionality helps explain why bias affects people differently.
