Tech Hero: Kimberly Bryant

Home About Implicit Bias Guide Tech Hero

Kimberly Bryant is a technologist and activist best known as the founder of Black Girls Code, a nonprofit organization dedicated to teaching coding and computer science skills to young Black girls. After working in biotechnology and engineering, Bryant recognized the lack of diversity in tech and decided to create pathways for students who are often excluded from these spaces.

Through workshops, summer camps, and community programs, Black Girls Code empowers students with both technical skills and confidence. Bryant’s work challenges systemic barriers related to race and gender while addressing implicit bias in education and hiring practices.

Her leadership demonstrates how technology can be used as a tool for social change, not just innovation. Rather than focusing on profit or prestige, Bryant centers access, representation, and long-term impact—making her an important figure in the movement toward equity in tech.
